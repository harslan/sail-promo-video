# Sawyer Business School Brand Video ‚Äî Creative Brief
## "Where Ownership Is Built"
### IMMERSE √ó SAIL ‚Äî Two Frameworks. One School.

---

## THE ASSIGNMENT

Build a **75-second cinematic brand video** for Suffolk University's Sawyer Business School. This is not a video about IMMERSE. This is not a video about SAIL. This is the video that shows how the two fit together ‚Äî and why that combination is something no other business school in the country can offer.

**Target audience:** Prospective undergraduate and graduate students, their parents, admissions counselors, conference audiences, accreditation reviewers, industry partners, and university leadership.

**The question this video answers:** "Why Suffolk?"

**The answer:** Because Suffolk has two nationally recognized, award-winning frameworks working in concert ‚Äî one for how you learn (IMMERSE), one for who you become (SAIL) ‚Äî and together they produce graduates who don't just understand AI, they own the outcomes of working with it.

## ASPECT RATIO & FORMAT

**Primary:**
- Resolution: 1920 x 1080 (16:9 horizontal ‚Äî this is a brand piece for presentations, website hero, conference screens)
- Frame rate: 30fps
- Duration: 2250 frames (75 seconds)
- Output: MP4

**Secondary:**
- Also create a 1080 x 1920 (9:16 vertical) version for social/mobile
- Adapted layouts, same content and timing

## THE STORY ARC (6 Scenes)

---

### Scene 1: THE GENERIC CLAIM (frames 0‚Äì210, 7 seconds)

**Concept:** Start with what every school says. Then break it.

Text appears in a clean, generic business-school style (deliberately sterile):

```
"Every business school talks about
real-world experience."
```

This should look like it could be from ANY school's brochure. Generic font treatment. Clean white-on-dark. Intentionally unremarkable.

Then ‚Äî a visual crack, glitch, or disruption. The generic text fragments and dissolves. We're done with the generic version.

**Tone:** Polished boredom ‚Üí disruption.

---

### Scene 2: THE IMMERSE REALITY (frames 210‚Äì840, 21 seconds)

**Concept:** Show what IMMERSE actually looks like. Fast, energetic, real. This should feel like a trailer for the most exciting school on earth.

**Opening beat (3 seconds):**
Text: "This is what it actually looks like."
‚Äî Sharp. Confident. Immediate.

**Then a rapid montage sequence (18 seconds):**

Each item gets 2-3 seconds. Represented as kinetic text + abstract visual motifs (since we're in Remotion, these are typographic/graphic, not photos):

1. **"Gillette Stadium"** ‚Äî Students consulting on sustainability at an NFL venue
   - Visual: Stadium silhouette + data visualization overlays
   
2. **"Boston Celtics"** ‚Äî Strategic partnership with an NBA franchise
   - Visual: Championship green accent + strategy diagrams
   
3. **"100,000 Real Orders"** ‚Äî Prompt AIChemy competition
   - Visual: Data stream flowing, numbers counting
   
4. **"Make-A-Wish"** ‚Äî Service learning with real nonprofit impact
   - Visual: Warm glow, connection lines
   
5. **"City of Boston"** ‚Äî Tackling wicked problems for the actual city
   - Visual: City skyline silhouette + problem-mapping nodes
   
6. **"Student-Run Agency"** ‚Äî Suffolk in the Hub, real clients, real campaigns
   - Visual: Creative burst, portfolio elements
   
7. **"Global Travel Seminars"** ‚Äî International immersive experiences
   - Visual: Globe/map with route lines

Each card/moment should have the IMMERSE framework letter it maps to in a subtle badge:
- Gillette ‚Üí [E] Experiential
- Celtics ‚Üí [S] Synergistic
- 100K Orders ‚Üí [I] Integrative
- Make-A-Wish ‚Üí [E] Emotional
- City of Boston ‚Üí [R] Relevant
- Student Agency ‚Üí [M] Motivational
- Global ‚Üí [M] Multisensory

**The montage should feel FAST, ALIVE, IMPRESSIVE.** This is the energy scene. The viewer should think: "Wait, they do ALL of that?"

At the end of the montage, a quick flash of all seven IMMERSE letters assembling:

**I ¬∑ M ¬∑ M ¬∑ E ¬∑ R ¬∑ S ¬∑ E**

With the tagline: "Immersive learning. Nationally recognized."
Smaller: "2024 Eduventures Innovation Award"

---

### Scene 3: THE PIVOT (frames 840‚Äì1050, 7 seconds)

**Concept:** The turn. Experience alone is not enough.

The energy pulls back. The screen calms. A single line appears:

```
"But experience without a framework
is just activity."
```

Hold for a beat. Let it land.

Then:

```
"What if the experience also developed
something AI cannot replicate?"
```

This is the bridge from IMMERSE to SAIL. The question that connects them.

**Tone:** Quiet. Thoughtful. The pause between the montage energy and the thesis.

---

### Scene 4: SAIL MEETS IMMERSE (frames 1050‚Äì1530, 16 seconds)

**Concept:** Show how SAIL competencies are forged inside IMMERSE experiences. This is the integration ‚Äî the thing no other school has.

**Opening (3 seconds):**
The four SAIL letters appear: **S ¬∑ A ¬∑ I ¬∑ L**
Below them: "AI Leadership Collaborative"
Smaller: "2025 Eduventures Innovation Award"

Two awards. Back to back. Let that register.

**Then four quick mappings (3 seconds each = 12 seconds):**

Each one shows a SAIL pillar paired with a real IMMERSE moment:

**S ‚Äî Social Intelligence**
"How do we connect?"
‚Üí "Presenting strategy to the Boston Celtics front office."
- Visual: The S letter + Celtics green accent + presentation motif
- The IMMERSE badge: [S] Synergistic

**A ‚Äî AI Literacy**
"How does it work?"
‚Üí "Analyzing 100,000 real orders with AI."
- Visual: The A letter + data flow + teal accent
- The IMMERSE badge: [I] Integrative

**I ‚Äî Innovation / Inquiry**
"What's missing or wrong?"
‚Üí "We need to make sure we're not penalizing rural sellers."
- Visual: The I letter + coral accent + magnifying lens
- The IMMERSE badge: [R] Relevant
- Subtle "‚Äî unprompted" label in coral

**L ‚Äî Leadership**
"I will own what happens."
‚Üí "Signing off on a recommendation to an actual client."
- Visual: The L letter + Suffolk gold + signature motif
- The IMMERSE badge: [E] Experiential
- This one should feel weightier. The gold is warmer. The animation is slower.

**The point:** Every SAIL competency is developed through an actual IMMERSE experience. Theory meets practice. Framework meets reality.

---

### Scene 5: THE THESIS (frames 1530‚Äì1830, 10 seconds)

**Concept:** The combined promise. The climax.

**Beat 1 (4 seconds):**
Both framework names appear side by side or stacked:

```
IMMERSE          SAIL
How you learn.   Who you become.
```

Connection lines form between them. They pulse together.

"Two award-winning frameworks. One school."

**Beat 2 (6 seconds):**
The thesis arrives in the three-beat rhythm:

```
"Skills can be automated."
"Judgment can be augmented."
"Ownership cannot."
```

Same treatment as the SAIL promo ‚Äî escalating size, the word "Ownership" in Suffolk gold. But this time, below it:

```
"And ownership is built here."
```

That last line ‚Äî "built here" ‚Äî is new. It only makes sense in the context of IMMERSE. Ownership isn't taught in a lecture. It's BUILT through immersive experiences with real stakes.

---

### Scene 6: THE CLOSE (frames 1830‚Äì2250, 14 seconds)

**Concept:** Institutional credibility + call to action.

**Beat 1 (5 seconds):**
```
Suffolk University
Sawyer Business School
Boston, MA
```

Credentials (elegant, spaced, not cluttered):
- "AACSB Accredited"
- "2024 Eduventures Innovation Award ‚Äî IMMERSE"
- "2025 Eduventures Innovation Award ‚Äî SAIL"
- "Published in AACSB Insights"

**Beat 2 (5 seconds):**
```
suffolk.edu/business
```
A heartbeat pulse animation (The Pulse motif ü´Ä).

**Beat 3 (4 seconds):**
Fade to black. The final lingering text:

```
Where ownership is built.
```

## CRITICAL CREATIVE DIRECTION

1. **Scene 2 is the energy peak.** The IMMERSE montage should feel fast, impressive, alive. Think movie trailer pacing. Every card should make the viewer go "they do THAT?" Stack the most impressive partnerships ‚Äî Celtics, Gillette, City of Boston ‚Äî these aren't made up.

2. **Scene 3 is the hinge.** The one line ‚Äî "But experience without a framework is just activity" ‚Äî turns the entire video from a marketing reel into an intellectual argument. It should feel like someone stopped the music.

3. **Scene 4 is the integration.** Each SAIL pillar paired with a real IMMERSE experience. This is the unique value proposition. This is what no other school can show. Make each mapping feel like a revelation.

4. **Scene 5 is the climax.** Two frameworks appearing together, then the three-beat thesis, then "And ownership is built here." That last line ‚Äî "built here" ‚Äî only works because the viewer has just watched 60 seconds of proof that Suffolk BUILDS things through immersion.

5. **The "‚Äî unprompted" moment in Scene 4** (Innovation/Inquiry pillar) should still be a visual event. That coral label. It carries the same emotional weight as in the SAIL promo.

6. **This is 16:9 horizontal (primary), not 9:16 vertical.** This plays on screens at conferences, on the website hero, in Board presentations. Design for widescreen first.

7. **Both IMMERSE and SAIL should feel equally important.** Neither framework is subordinate. They are PARTNERS. Two columns of the same building.

8. **The consecutive awards are a flex. Let them flex.** "2024 Eduventures Innovation Award" and "2025 Eduventures Innovation Award" appearing together ‚Äî back to back years ‚Äî is powerful social proof. Don't bury it.

- Cards should overlap transitions slightly (20 frame crossfade)

- End with a rapid-fire 30-frame flash of all IMMERSE letters assembling
